Professional Summary

Ph.D. student in Mechanical Engineering specializing in Automated Fiber Placement (AFP) and composite manufacturing. I develop multiphysics FEA models for resin flow, compaction, and cure kinetics, validated through DIC, microscopy, and mechanical testing. My work includes progressive failure analysis using open-source tools like COMPDAM and custom 3D Hashin-based models. I also investigate the effects of thermoplastic veils and other materials on improving interlaminar toughness. My goal is to optimize AFP processing and material performance through simulation-driven design and experimental validation.

Research Projects

  • AFP Defect Modeling: Simulated resin-rich regions and tow gap behavior using multiphysics FEA; validated with tensile and DIC data.
  • Resin Flow in Microchannels: Conducted rheometry to inform predictive models of two-phase flow.
  • Thermoplastic Veils for Waviness Reduction: Placed PEI/PPS veils in AFP layups; achieved up to 90% waviness reduction.
  • Residual Stress Prediction: Developed an integrative framework using FBG sensors, DSC, DMA, and thermal simulations.
  • Biomass-Based Graphene in CFRP: Evaluated mechanical performance and failure behavior of modified composites.
  • Graphene Yield Optimization: Analyzed pGNP yield using SEM, TGA, and Raman spectroscopy.
  • Financial Time Series Analysis: Used R to preprocess, calibrate, and model economic datasets.
