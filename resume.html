About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at Old Dominion University, specializing in AFP-based composite structures. My research involves extensive experimental work using techniques such as Digital Image Correlation (DIC), Dynamic Mechanical Analysis (DMA), mechanical testing with MTS systems, Differential Scanning Calorimetry (DSC), Dielectric Analysis (DEA), micrography, and micro-CT scanning. These methods allow me to rigorously characterize material behavior, evaluate internal morphologies, and validate structural responses of composites under different manufacturing and loading conditions. Alongside experimentation, I have a strong passion for modeling and simulation. I focus on developing high-fidelity models that accurately replicate the physical behavior of composite materials. My simulation work includes resin-rich morphology prediction, flow-compaction interaction analysis, and AI-based reconstruction. I am particularly interested in the dual nature of composites—studying both the mechanical behavior of structures and the flow of resins during manufacturing. This drives my commitment to deepening my expertise in both solid mechanics and fluid dynamics to enable more predictive and comprehensive composite modeling.
